These dossiers expand the seeded patent register into research-ready records. They are not the complete Steinmetz patent corpus. They are the first public control layer for patents currently listed in the intake register.

Every mature patent page must eventually include the original PDF, drawing extraction, claim transcription, checksum, and page-image references. Until then, each record below is a structured dossier, not a final legal or historical analysis.

## Reading Rule

A patent tells us what apparatus or method was legally claimed. It does not automatically tell us Steinmetz's full theoretical philosophy. Use patents as engineering evidence and compare them with books only after the overlap is source-located.

## US533244A: System of Distribution by Alternating Currents

- Patent link: [Google Patents](https://patents.google.com/patent/US533244A/en)
- PDF: [US533244.pdf](https://patentimages.storage.googleapis.com/pdfs/US533244.pdf)
- Publication date: 1895-01-29
- Filing date: 1894-04-02
- Assignee: General Electric Company
- Main domain: alternating-current distribution, phase displacement, monocyclic/polyphase service

Technical digest: a monocyclic AC distribution system using a supplementary phase-modifying source or coil so ordinary single-phase lighting loads and polyphase motor loads can be supplied from a common system.

Archive value: this belongs beside symbolic AC, phase relation, polyphase currents, and early AC distribution history. The drawing extraction pass should preserve the distribution system diagram, generator bank diagram, phase-modifying coil regulation diagram, and geometrical phase-displacement diagram.

## Patent Promotion Checklist

Each seeded dossier becomes a mature patent page only after:

1. PDF downloaded into `sources/steinmetz_patents/raw/<patent-number>/`.
2. PDF checksum recorded.
3. Claims and abstract transcribed or OCR-corrected.
4. Drawings extracted as original figure assets.
5. Apparatus explanation written from the patent itself.
6. Modern engineering reading added as a separate layer.
7. Related Steinmetz book passages linked by source location.
8. Speculative or ether-field interpretation, if any, kept separate from patent fact.
