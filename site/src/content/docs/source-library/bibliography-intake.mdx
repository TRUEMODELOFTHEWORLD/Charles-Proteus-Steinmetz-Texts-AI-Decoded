This page turns the current Wikipedia bibliography into an acquisition and processing control list. It is not the final scholarly bibliography. It is the work queue that prevents the archive from forgetting an important Steinmetz source while we continue scan-by-scan verification.

Source basis: [Wikipedia, Charles Proteus Steinmetz, Bibliography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Proteus_Steinmetz#Bibliography). Every listed work below still requires primary source verification before its claims are promoted as reviewed.

## Status Rule

- `Seeded`: a local source record and public page already exist.
- `Edition variant seeded`: the archive has a related edition, but edition/year differences must remain visible.
- `Planned source acquisition`: acquire the book, lecture collection, or pamphlet.
- `Planned article acquisition`: acquire the paper, OCR it, extract equations, figures, concepts, terms, and hidden gems.
- `Posthumous or late publication candidate`: verify chronology before interpreting the work historically.

## Promotion Pipeline

For every work above:

1. Acquire a primary scan or publication record.
2. Store raw files, OCR, and custody notes under `sources/`.
3. Generate source manifest, checksum, page map, OCR text, figures, equations, glossary candidates, concept tags, quotes, annotations, and crosslinks.
4. Promote public pages only after the source location is visible.
5. Keep modern explanation, mathematical reconstruction, historical context, and ether-field interpretation in separate labeled layers.
