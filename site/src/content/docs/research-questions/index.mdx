## Open Questions

1. Did Steinmetz use field language differently from modern textbooks?
2. Did his mathematical treatment preserve physical meaning that later teaching abstracts away?
3. Are there concepts in his transient analysis that are under-taught today?
4. How does his treatment of hysteresis compare to modern magnetic material theory?
5. Did he explicitly reference ether, and in what technical contexts?
6. Are there links between his symbolic method and later phasor engineering?
7. Where did Tesla and Steinmetz agree or diverge?
8. How can Wheeler-style readings be represented without confusing interpretation with historical fact?
9. How did Steinmetz's distinction between resistance and reactance shape later AC pedagogy?
10. Does the older term "condensive reactance" preserve a useful physical picture that "capacitive reactance" partly hides?
11. Where do transient terms in Steinmetz's writing anticipate modern surge-protection and insulation-coordination practice?
12. Which original AC and transient diagrams should be promoted first for the public site?
