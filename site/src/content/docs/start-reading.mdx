## A Good First Hour

1. Read [Who Was Steinmetz?](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/who-was-steinmetz/) to get the human and technical map.
2. Open [Radiation, Light and Illumination](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/book-coverage/radiation-light-and-illumination/) and choose a lecture.
3. Click `Read the source text` if you want the original flow, or `workbench` if you want concepts, terms, equations, figures, and review prompts.
4. Keep the lower-right `Steinmetz only` control in mind when you want to hide modern and interpretive layers.
5. Use [Concept Concordance](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/concept-concordance/) when a word catches your attention and you want to see where else it appears, or [Theme Evidence Atlas](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/theme-evidence/) when you want a whole research theme gathered at once.

## The Rule Of The Archive

This project welcomes multiple ways of reading Steinmetz, but it does not blur the layers. A source claim, a modern engineering translation, a mathematical reconstruction, a Tesla-era comparison, and an ether-field interpretation are different kinds of statements. The site is designed so each can be useful without pretending to be the others.
