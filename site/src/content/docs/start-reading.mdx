## A Good First Sitting

1. Read [Who Was Steinmetz?](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/who-was-steinmetz/) if you do not already know the man and his role in electrical engineering.
2. Read [Radiation, Light and Illumination, Lecture I](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/source-texts/radiation-light-and-illumination/lecture-01/) as primary text.
3. Open the [Lecture I deep-decoding page](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/sources/radiation-light-and-illumination/lecture-01/) only after you have a feel for Steinmetz's own language.
4. Visit [Radiation](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/concepts/radiation/), [Electric Waves](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/concepts/electric-waves/), and [Ether](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/concepts/ether/) when a concept catches your attention.
5. Use [Guided Reading Routes](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/reading-routes/) after the first sitting, when you know whether you want AC theory, transients, field language, diagrams, mathematics, apparatus, or patents.
6. Use the [Research Map](/Charles-Proteus-Steinmetz-Texts-AI-Decoded/research-map/) when you want one page that joins books, concepts, passages, and review layers.

## How The Archive Is Layered

The site is intentionally strict about labels:

- **Source text** means Steinmetz's own processed text or a scan-derived asset.
- **Workbench** means a research map: concepts, glossary terms, equations, figures, quotes, and next verification tasks.
- **Modern explanation** means present electrical-engineering or physics language.
- **Interpretive reading** means a clearly labeled reading, including ether-field or Wheeler-style comparisons.
- **Verification pages** are for turning candidate OCR and candidate equations into reliable scholarly material.

Use the small **Reader Mode** control in the sidebar when you want to switch between all layers and source-only reading.
