The archive may eventually grow beyond Steinmetz into a wider research library for classical electrical science, alternative cosmology, field interpretations, geometry, and disputed models. That future is exciting, but the current priority remains the Steinmetz archive.

This page records the architecture principle now so the system can grow without mixing sources, claims, and interpretations.

## Primary Rule

Source isolation comes before synthesis.

Every claim should carry enough metadata to answer:

- Who said it?
- In what source?
- On what page or in what claim?
- Is it an explicit source claim, modern explanation, mathematical reconstruction, historical note, interpretive reading, speculative connection, or unresolved question?
- Has it been verified against a scan or authority record?

## Attribution Layers

When multiple figures address the same concept, a mature concept page should keep the voices separate:

- Steinmetz explicitly states
- Tesla explicitly states
- Walter Russell explicitly states
- Eric Dollard explicitly states
- Ken Wheeler explicitly states
- Modern scientific view
- Mathematical reconstruction
- Interpretive synthesis
- Speculative or unresolved notes

The synthesis layer can be bold, but it must never pretend to be a source quotation.
