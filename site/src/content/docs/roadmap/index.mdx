## Goal

The immediate goal is a live, useful Steinmetz research archive, not a finished encyclopedia. A rigorous public resource can go live while clearly marking which layers are source-verified, candidate, interpretive, or still pending.

## Live-Ready Core

The archive is live-ready when these conditions are met:

- The source library is visible and honest about processing state.
- The first book has original scan crops, source overview, lecture map, and at least one deep lecture page.
- The AC source has symbolic method, impedance, reactance, admittance, and power-factor entry points.
- The transient source has permanent/transient term, oscillation, damping, surge, and distributed-line entry points.
- The glossary explains obsolete terms such as condensive reactance and wattless component.
- Interpretive pages are clearly separated from historical and mathematical claims.
- GitHub Pages deploys automatically from `main`.

Move quickly by publishing labeled layers, not by lowering standards. Candidate pages are allowed. Unlabeled certainty is not.
