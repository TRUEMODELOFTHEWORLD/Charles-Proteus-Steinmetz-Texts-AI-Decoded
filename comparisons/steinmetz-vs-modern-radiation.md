# Steinmetz vs Modern Electrical Engineering: Radiation

Status: seeded.

## What Steinmetz Says

In `Radiation, Light and Illumination`, Lecture I, Steinmetz frames radiation as energy in transit and criticizes the phrase "radiant heat" when it obscures the distinction between radiation and heat produced by absorption.

## What Modern Textbooks Say

Modern physics and electrical engineering describe electromagnetic radiation as field energy propagating through space. Heat is commonly treated as energy transfer due to temperature difference; thermal radiation is one mode of that transfer.

## Are They Equivalent?

Mostly yes. Steinmetz's wording is historically older but conceptually strong. His language makes the energy-state transition clearer than many simplified explanations.

## What Modern Language May Hide

Modern language can become too abstract when it jumps straight to rays, photons, or field equations without emphasizing the emitter-transit-absorber chain.

## Ether-Field Interpretive Reading

Interpretive only: Steinmetz's framing supports a field-centered reading of radiation, especially when read alongside his ether passage. The interpretive layer should not claim more than the text supports.
