# Radiation

Status: seeded from `radiation-light-and-illumination`; cross-source review pending.

## Core Definition

Radiation is energy in transit between an emitter and an absorber. Steinmetz's first lecture uses this distinction to separate radiation from heat: radiation may become heat when absorbed, but it is not heat while traveling.

## Source Appearances

- `radiation-light-and-illumination`, Lecture I.

## Modern Electrical Engineering Reading

Radiation is electromagnetic energy propagation. Depending on frequency and context, it may be treated as radio waves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, or circuit-field propagation.
