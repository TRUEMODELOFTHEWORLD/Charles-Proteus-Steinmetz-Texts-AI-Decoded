# Lightning And Surges

Source status: OCR candidate; scan verification required.

Primary source anchors:

- `Theory and Calculation of Transient Electric Phenomena and Oscillations`, transient-line, standing-wave, traveling-wave, high-potential, and surge chapters.
- `Elementary Lectures on Electric Discharges, Waves and Impulses`, discharge and impulse passages.

Steinmetz treats lightning and high-potential disturbances as transient phenomena where the simple lumped-circuit view is insufficient. Distance, propagation velocity, line constants, reflection, insulation stress, and stored field energy become central.

Modern equivalent:

- Switching surge and lightning impulse analysis.
- Transmission-line traveling waves.
- Surge impedance and reflection coefficient.
- Insulation coordination and overvoltage protection.

Interpretive boundary:

Surges are naturally attractive to field-centered interpretation because they expose propagation, reflection, stored energy, and dielectric stress. Such readings must remain separated from source statements and modern engineering formulas.
