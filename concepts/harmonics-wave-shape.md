# Harmonics And Wave Shape

Source status: OCR candidate; scan verification required.

Primary source anchors:

- `Theory and Calculation of Alternating Current Phenomena`, candidate chapters on higher harmonics, wave-shape distortion, and effective values.
- `Engineering Mathematics`, trigonometric series and practical curve decomposition.

Steinmetz treats alternating-current waves as practical engineering curves, not only ideal sine waves. Higher harmonics can change peak value, effective value, transformer loss, hysteresis loss, and machine behavior.

Modern equivalent:

- Fourier components.
- Harmonic distortion.
- RMS and crest factor.
- Transformer and machine loss under nonsinusoidal excitation.

Interpretive boundary:

Wave-shape analysis may interest field-centered readers because it keeps temporal structure visible. That does not make any later interpretation a historical claim about Steinmetz's ontology.
