# Conductance

Conductance is the active or power component of admittance.

Source-located candidate anchors:

- `Theory and Calculation of Alternating Current Phenomena`, Chapter VIII, admittance/conductance/susceptance passages.
- `Theory and Calculation of Alternating Current Phenomena`, Chapter XIV, dielectric-loss passages where conductance represents leakage and energy loss.

Modern equivalent:

```math
G = \Re(Y)
```

In a pure resistor `G = 1/R`; in a general AC network conductance is the real component of the reciprocal of the full impedance.
