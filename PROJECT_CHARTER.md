# Project Charter

## Purpose

Steinmetz Decoded exists to recover, preserve, and explain Charles Proteus Steinmetz's electrical writings with a level of care that serves engineers, historians, physicists, electrical theory students, Tesla researchers, and open-minded field/ether researchers.

The archive must remain rigorous without becoming narrow, and open-minded without becoming careless.

## Research Principles

1. Preserve the source before interpreting it.
2. Keep original wording, notation, page references, figures, and historical vocabulary visible.
3. Translate old terminology into modern language without erasing the old conceptual structure.
4. Separate fact, derivation, context, and interpretation with explicit labels.
5. Treat nonstandard interpretations as research readings, not as historical proof.
6. Prefer exact citations, page maps, and source-linked statements over attractive summaries.
7. Mark uncertainty early and visibly.

## Interpretation Layers

Every major concept page should eventually include:

- Steinmetz original definition or usage.
- Source appearances and citations.
- Modern electrical engineering interpretation.
- Mathematical breakdown and notation translation.
- Plain-English explanation.
- Historical context.
- Tesla-era comparison when relevant.
- Ether-field interpretive reading when relevant.
- Current status: still used, renamed, discarded, absorbed, or misunderstood.

## Anti-Hallucination Rules

- Do not present an interpretation as a source claim.
- Do not infer a Tesla connection without showing the textual or technical bridge.
- Do not infer a Wheeler-style reading without labeling it interpretive.
- Do not silently modernize Steinmetz's notation.
- Do not quote OCR without checking it against the scan when the quote matters.
- Do not promote extracted candidates to canonical facts until reviewed.

## Canonical Review States

- `raw`: source artifact copied or linked, not interpreted.
- `ocr`: machine text exists but is not corrected.
- `candidate`: extraction produced by script or first-pass reading.
- `reviewed`: human or model-assisted review checked against source scan.
- `canonical`: promoted to stable public knowledge page with source citation.
- `needs-verification`: useful but not yet safe to rely on.

## Tone

The archive should be reverent toward Steinmetz's genius, technically serious, historically careful, mathematically explicit, and readable enough to invite non-specialists into the work.
