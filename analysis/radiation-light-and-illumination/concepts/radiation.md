## Steinmetz's Own Meaning

Steinmetz treats radiation as a form of energy in transit. It can be produced from other energy forms and converted back into other forms. His immediate example is an incandescent lamp: electrical energy heats the filament, the filament emits radiation, and an absorbing body converts the intercepted radiation into heat.

## Modern Equivalent

Modern physics would describe this as electromagnetic radiation carrying energy through space and transferring energy to matter upon absorption. The modern language would usually distinguish thermal radiation from heat transfer, while Steinmetz sharply warns that the radiation itself is not heat before absorption.

## Mathematical Layer

Radiation in Lecture I is organized by:

```math
S = f \lambda
```

The same relation connects radio waves, light, ultraviolet radiation, and other regions of the spectrum.

## Plain-English Explanation

Radiation is not the hot object and not the final warming effect. It is the energy traveling between emitter and absorber.

## Historical Context

This wording comes from a period when engineers were unifying optics, wireless telegraphy, and alternating-current field theory under Maxwellian electromagnetic concepts, while still using ether language inherited from nineteenth-century wave theory.

## Ether-Field Interpretive Reading

Interpretive only: Steinmetz's insistence that radiation is energy traveling through a field-bearing medium can be read as a field-first account rather than a particle-first account. A Wheeler-style reading may emphasize energy transfer through dielectric/magnetic field conditions, but that is a modern nonstandard interpretation layered onto Steinmetz's text.

## Current Status

The core idea is still used. The term "radiant heat" survives colloquially, but technical teaching usually distinguishes radiation from heat as energy transfer by electromagnetic waves.
