## Steinmetz's Explicit Claim

In Lecture I, Steinmetz introduces ether as the name given to the hypothetical medium required by light-as-wave-motion reasoning. He links its supposed properties to the high velocity of radiation and to the fact that light passes through vacuum.

## Modern Equivalent

Modern electromagnetic theory does not require a mechanical luminiferous ether. Electromagnetic waves are described as field solutions propagating through spacetime. The historical ether language is important, but it cannot be read as modern proof of an ether ontology.

## Historical Context

The book was published in 1909, after Maxwell and Hertz and during the period when relativity and field theory were changing the status of ether language. Engineers often retained older terms while using mathematically modern electromagnetic results.

## Ether-Field Interpretive Reading
