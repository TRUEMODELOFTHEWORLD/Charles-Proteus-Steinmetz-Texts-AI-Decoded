## Steinmetz's Usage

Steinmetz presents electric waves as very long waves used in wireless telegraphy. He then extends the discussion downward to alternating-current circuit fields and upward to Hertzian waves and high-frequency discharges.

## Modern Equivalent

The modern equivalent is radio-frequency electromagnetic waves, plus the broader idea that time-varying electric and magnetic fields propagate at finite speed. In circuit theory, the approximation used depends on whether the circuit dimensions are small relative to wavelength.

## Mathematical Layer

The relation is:

```math
\lambda = \frac{S}{f}
```

For `f = 60 Hz` and `S = 3 x 10^10 cm/s`, the wavelength is about `5 x 10^8 cm`, or about `3,100 miles`.

## Why It Matters

This passage connects power engineering, radio, lightning-arrester oscillations, and optics on one frequency scale. It is a useful bridge between Steinmetz's AC/transient work and his treatment of radiation.
