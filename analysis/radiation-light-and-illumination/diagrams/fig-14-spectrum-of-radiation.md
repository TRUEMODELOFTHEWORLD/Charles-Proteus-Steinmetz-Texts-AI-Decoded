## What Steinmetz Is Showing

Steinmetz arranges radiations by frequency and wavelength, emphasizing that visible light is only a small region in a much broader radiation spectrum. Electric waves occupy the long-wave, low-frequency side; ultraviolet and X-rays occupy higher-frequency regions.

## Modern Annotation

This is a historical electromagnetic spectrum chart. Some terminology differs from modern usage: "ultra-red" corresponds to infrared, and "electric waves" overlaps with what modern readers would usually call radio-frequency electromagnetic waves.
