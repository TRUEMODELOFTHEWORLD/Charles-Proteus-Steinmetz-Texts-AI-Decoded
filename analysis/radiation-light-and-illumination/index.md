## Why This Source Matters

This book is not one of Steinmetz's AC circuit classics, but it is a powerful first source for the archive because it exposes his broader physical language. He treats radiation, light, heat, electric waves, perception, photometry, arc lighting, and illumination as an engineering continuum.

Lecture I is especially important for this project because it contains explicit language about radiation as energy, the relation between light waves and electric waves, the velocity of propagation, and the historical ether as the hypothesized carrier or seat of radiative energy.

## Key Initial Concepts

- Radiation as energy.
- Heat radiation versus radiation energy.
- Light as the most conspicuous form of radiation.
- Wave theory versus corpuscular theory.
- Frequency, wavelength, and propagation velocity.
- The ether as historical field-bearing medium.
- Electric waves as long-wave members of the radiation spectrum.
- Alternating-current fields as waves whose wavelength is usually too long for propagation effects to dominate ordinary circuit dimensions.

## Interpretation Discipline

The book gives real source material for field and ether interpretation, but it must be handled carefully. Steinmetz explicitly uses ether language in Lecture I; that does not automatically validate any later ether ontology. The archive should preserve the exact historical wording, translate the physics into modern electromagnetic language, and then add a clearly labeled ether-field interpretive reading.
