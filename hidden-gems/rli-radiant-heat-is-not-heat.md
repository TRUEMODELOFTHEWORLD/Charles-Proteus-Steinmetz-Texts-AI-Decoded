# Radiant Heat Is Not Heat

## Quote or Paraphrase

Steinmetz argues that so-called radiant heat is radiation energy while in transit and becomes heat only when intercepted and absorbed.

## Why It Matters

This is a clean example of Steinmetz refusing sloppy physical language. It helps readers distinguish between energy propagation and the later thermal effect at the absorbing body.

## Modern Interpretation

Modern physics agrees with the distinction: electromagnetic radiation can transfer energy; heat is not a material fluid traveling through space.

## Possible Forgotten Implication

The passage encourages field-process thinking. It asks the reader to track what the energy is doing at each stage rather than relying on common-language labels.
