## Quote or Paraphrase

Steinmetz explains that the magnetic and electrostatic fields of alternating-current circuits progress outward as radiation waves, but their wavelengths are so large at low frequency that propagation is usually not considered.

## Why It Matters

This is a bridge between ordinary circuit theory and field/wave theory. It also anticipates why distributed effects matter in long lines, high frequencies, lightning surges, and fast transients.

## Modern Interpretation

Modern engineers use lumped circuit models when circuit dimensions are small compared with wavelength, and transmission-line or field models when that approximation fails.

## Ether-Field Connection

Interpretive only: This passage is important for field-first readings because it refuses to treat circuit fields as merely local bookkeeping quantities. They have extension and propagation, even when ordinary engineering approximations suppress that fact.
